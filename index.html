TTC Traffic Collisions and Subway Delay Analysis

In this project, I explored Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) data to see if traffic collisions correlate with subway delays. Using SQL for data preparation and Tableau for visualization, I drilled down into hourly and monthly patterns to separate fact from assumption.

Key Findings

  • Traffic collisions peak during rush hours (8 AM and 5 PM), but subway delays tend to increase at 6 AM and 10 PM.
  • No strong correlation was found between traffic collisions and subway delays, challenging the assumption that traffic directly affects subway performance.
  • Subway delays appear more related to internal factors like maintenance and equipment issues rather than external traffic conditions.

Purpose: Compare hourly collision counts with subway delay occurrences.

Observation:

  • Collisions: Peak at 8 AM and 5 PM (rush hours).
  • Delays: Peak at 6 AM and 10 PM, differing from collision peaks.

Insight: The mismatch in peak times suggests delays are not directly caused by traffic collisions, implying internal operational issues.

Purpose: Show the distribution of average subway delays throughout the day.

Observation:

  • Delays: Highest in early morning (around 6 AM) and late evening (around 10 PM).

Insight: Non-peak hours see increased delays, potentially due to maintenance or operational strategies occurring outside rush hours.

Purpose: Examine if monthly changes in collisions align with changes in subway delays.

Observation:

  • Fluctuations in collisions do not mirror trends in subway delays over months.

Insight: The lack of correlation across months supports the idea that collisions are not a main driver of subway delays.

Purpose: Visualize the correlation between collision frequency and subway delay duration on an hourly basis.

Observation:

  • The scatter plot shows no clear relationship or trend between the two variables.

Insight: Traffic collisions are likely not a significant factor causing subway delays, directing attention to internal issues.

Tools Used

  • SQL for Data Preparation
  • Tableau for Data Visualization

Conclusion: Subway delays are not strongly influenced by traffic collisions. By focusing on internal operational improvements, the TTC could potentially reduce delays and enhance the passenger experience.

Google Capstone Project: Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

For this capstone project, I analyzed Cyclistic's bike-share data to understand the riding habits of casual users versus annual members. The goal was to pinpoint marketing and operational strategies to convert occasional riders into loyal subscribers.

Key Findings

  • Casual riders tend to enjoy longer, more leisurely rides, often on weekends and during peak summer months.
  • Annual members prefer shorter, more frequent rides, spread consistently throughout the week, reflecting a more utility-driven approach.

Purpose: Compares the average ride lengths for two types of users: casual riders and members.

Observation:

  • Casual Riders: Average ride length is around 24.65 minutes, longer and less frequent.
  • Members: Average ride length is about 12.67 minutes, shorter and more frequent.

Insight: Casual riders likely use the service for leisurely, less frequent trips, while members use it for shorter, frequent trips—indicating a more utilitarian approach.

Purpose: Show seasonal trends in ride frequency throughout the year.

Observation:

  • Higher ride volumes occur in warmer months, especially among casual riders.

Insight: Seasonal surges suggest targeted promotions during summer months could convert casual users into annual members when interest is at its peak.

Purpose: Compare weekday vs. weekend usage patterns for casual riders and members.

Observation:

  • Casual Riders: Spike on weekends, indicating leisure-oriented usage.
  • Members: More consistent usage across weekdays, reflecting routine commuting.

Insight: Weekend promotions could entice casual riders to consider memberships. Consistent weekday usage by members supports their role as regular commuters.

Tools Used

  • Excel for Data Cleaning
  • Tableau for Visualization

Conclusion: By tailoring marketing initiatives to highlight membership perks—especially during weekend and holiday periods—Cyclistic can convert more casual riders into annual members and foster long-term loyalty.
