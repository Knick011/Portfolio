Financial Analysis: Meme Stocks vs. WMT, GOLD, and USD

Below is my most recent project, examining the volatile world of meme stocks like AMC, GME, and MSTR, contrasted against more established assets (WMT, GOLD, and USD). This analysis illustrates how simplistic visualizations (as seen in the first two charts) can mislead investors, whereas detailed, contextualized figures (the subsequent charts) provide clearer insights and more accurate conclusions.

The initial charts—Comparison of Meme Stocks and WMT and Average Price of Meme Stocks vs. Gold and USD—show how oversimplified or incomplete presentations can lead us to wrong assumptions. In contrast, the later figures illustrate best practices in financial analysis and data storytelling, covering monthly averages, investment metrics, outperformance, and volatility comparisons.

Comparison of Meme Stocks and WMT

Description: This line graph tracks the price trends of AMC, GME, MSTR (MicroStrategy), and WMT (Walmart) from early 2020 to 2025. Meme stocks like AMC and GME show extreme volatility, particularly during the 2021 meme stock rally. In contrast, WMT reflects a stable, blue-chip trajectory.

Analysis:

  • Key Peaks: GME spiked dramatically in early 2021, driven by a retail trading frenzy (e.g., Reddit’s WallStreetBets). AMC followed a similar, albeit less intense, pattern. MSTR’s performance often correlates with Bitcoin investments.
  • WMT’s Stability: Walmart exhibits gradual upward movement, emphasizing its role as a fundamentally driven stock.
  • General Trends: Meme stocks often lost much of their value post-peak, highlighting high risk-reward cycles. WMT’s resilience appeals to long-term, risk-averse investors.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Add annotations for key events (e.g., GME short squeeze in January 2021).
  • Consider a secondary Y-axis so WMT’s relatively smaller changes remain visible.
  • Apply color coding or shading to highlight periods of extreme volatility.

Average Price of Meme Stocks vs. Gold and USD

Description: This bar chart displays the average prices of AMC, GME, MSTR, WMT, Gold, and USD over the observed period. Gold ($1,944.59) stands out with a consistently high value, while meme stocks and WMT appear much lower. USD remains around $1.04.

Analysis:

  • Gold’s Role: Gold’s high average price reflects its status as a safe-haven asset, maintaining strong demand during economic uncertainty.
  • Meme Stocks vs. WMT: AMC, GME, and MSTR show higher averages than WMT due to sharp meme-fueled spikes. WMT’s lower but steadier trend offers reliability for investors.
  • USD: The chart shows the dollar’s value is relatively stable over time, underlining its role as a global reserve currency.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Normalize data as percentage returns instead of raw prices to make cross-asset comparisons more meaningful.
  • Include standard deviation or error bars to highlight each asset’s volatility.

As shown above, these simpler visuals can hide essential volatility and timing details. Let’s delve deeper into more nuanced analyses, revealing how additional context significantly improves our understanding.

Monthly Averages of Meme Stocks vs. Gold and USD

Description: This line graph tracks monthly average prices for the same assets, revealing broader trends over time. Gold maintains a relatively stable upward path, while meme stocks show drastic shifts coinciding with retail trading hype.

Analysis:

  • Meme Stock Volatility: GME and AMC soared in early 2021, then declined steeply as retail enthusiasm cooled. MSTR’s price moves correlate strongly with Bitcoin sentiments.
  • Gold and USD: Gold remains steady with slight upward momentum, while USD shows no distinct trend as it serves primarily as a currency measure.
  • WMT’s Performance: Walmart exhibits slow but steady growth, a hallmark of a mature, stable company.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Annotate specific months (e.g., GME’s surge in January 2021) for clarity.
  • Use a logarithmic scale to compare assets with vastly different price ranges.
  • Offer interactive toggles so viewers can isolate individual stock trends without clutter.

Investment Metric per USD Invested

Description: This visualization shows the growth (or decline) of 1 USD invested in each asset over time, highlighting relative returns rather than absolute prices.

Analysis:

  • GME’s Surge: The early 2021 short squeeze propelled GME to extraordinary returns, eventually reverting to more typical levels.
  • Steady Performers: WMT and Gold deliver modest yet consistent growth, appealing to investors valuing stability over speculation.
  • AMC and MSTR: Although they experience periodic spikes, both ultimately underperform WMT and Gold on a risk-adjusted basis.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Use a logarithmic Y-axis scale to better visualize smaller changes once large spikes appear.
  • Include additional comparison metrics (e.g., cumulative returns, Sharpe ratio) for deeper investor insights.

Meme Stocks Outperformance Percentage to WMT

Description: This bar chart indicates the percentage of days (or trading periods) in which each meme stock’s price closed above WMT’s performance baseline.

Analysis:

  • GME and MSTR: GME’s figure (~87%) and MSTR (~83%) highlight how extreme spikes can overshadow a stable stock like WMT, albeit for potentially short windows.
  • AMC: Underperforms GME and MSTR, reflecting less dramatic rallies.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Overlay a timeline to show when outperformance occurred, not just how often.
  • Add historical context or comparisons to other blue-chip stocks for benchmark analysis.

Monthly Ranking Based on Value

Description: This dual-view chart presents monthly rankings alongside average percentage increases for each asset, visualizing both absolute and relative performance.

Analysis:

  • Dominant Surges: GME tops the ranking in early 2021, correlating with its short-squeeze period.
  • Consistent but Lower: WMT remains lower in rank, reflecting reliable but unspectacular returns.
  • Volatility: AMC and GME rankings swing widely, underscoring the erratic nature of speculative stocks.

Suggestions for Improvement:

  • Consider splitting ranking and percentage increase into separate visuals to reduce cognitive load.
  • Use a heatmap approach to depict rank changes over time in a more compact format.

Volatility of Stocks in Relation to Gold

Description: This chart quantifies the percentage of time each stock’s price movement direction (up or down) aligns with Gold’s movement in the same period.

Analysis:

  • Low Correlation for Meme Stocks: AMC, GME, and MSTR show little overlap with Gold’s behavior, indicating speculative dynamics largely detached from traditional safe-haven movements.

Volatility of Stocks in Relation to USD

Description: Like the previous chart, this visual shows each asset’s alignment with USD trends over the same timeframe, measuring how often they move in the same direction.

  • WMT: Shows relatively higher alignment, indicating it's more sensitive to broader economic indicators that also affect USD.

By comparing simpler visuals to more comprehensive analyses, we see how critical in-depth data visualization and context can be. The final figures—highlighting monthly behavior, returns per dollar, and volatility—offer a far more accurate understanding of risk and performance than a basic average price or raw line chart.

TTC Traffic Collisions and Subway Delay Analysis

In this analysis, I investigated whether traffic collisions in Toronto correlate with subway delays on the Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) network. By cleaning and preparing data in SQL, then creating interactive visuals in Tableau, I sought to uncover any meaningful link between surface-level traffic collisions and underground subway disruptions.

Approach

  • Data Collection & Cleaning: Imported collision records and TTC delay logs, using SQL to filter out incomplete data, remove duplicates, and standardize date/time formats for accurate comparison.
  • Data Analysis: Grouped collision counts by hour and month, while examining average subway delay durations during the same periods. Calculated correlation coefficients to gauge the relationship between collisions and delays.
  • Visualization: Built interactive Tableau dashboards to visualize hourly/monthly trends and identify potential overlap between traffic collisions and subway delays.

Key Findings

  • Distinct Rush-Hour Patterns: Traffic collisions peaked during traditional rush hours (8 AM, 5 PM), but subway delays spiked at 6 AM and 10 PM, suggesting different driving factors.
  • No Strong Correlation: Monthly and hourly analyses showed minimal alignment between collision numbers and subway delays.
  • Operational Influences on Delays: Maintenance and equipment issues appeared more responsible for delays than external traffic conditions, particularly during off-peak hours.

Purpose: Illustrate and compare the frequency of traffic collisions against subway delay occurrences on an hourly basis.

Observation:

  • Collisions peak around rush hours (8 AM and 5 PM).
  • Subway delays peak at 6 AM and 10 PM, outside typical rush-hour windows.

Insight: The divergence in peak times underscores the limited influence of rush-hour collisions on subway disruptions.

Additional Detail: The line chart aggregates total collisions and delay counts by hour, allowing a direct visual comparison of trends throughout a 24-hour period.

Purpose: Highlight the hours with the longest average subway delay durations.

Observation:

  • Average delays are highest early in the morning (around 6 AM) and late in the evening (around 10 PM).

Insight: Off-peak hours may be prone to longer delays due to planned maintenance, fewer staff on duty, or scheduled operational tasks.

Additional Detail: The data is aggregated by hour and averaged to reveal when commuters might experience the greatest delays, guiding resource allocation.

Purpose: Examine whether monthly fluctuations in collision frequency mirror subway delay trends.

Observation:

  • Months with high collision counts do not necessarily see a spike in subway delays.
  • Seasonal variation in traffic accidents does not translate directly to subway service disruptions.

Insight: Seasonal factors affecting road safety (e.g., winter conditions) do not appear to consistently impact underground rail service.

Additional Detail: This view helps identify potential seasonal or weather-related factors. While collisions may follow certain monthly patterns, they do not align with subway performance issues over the same timeframe.

Purpose of the Visualization:

  • Provide an hourly analysis of traffic collision frequency (rectangle size) and average subway delays (color intensity).
  • Enable a quick visual comparison of how collisions and delays vary by hour of the day.

How to Read It:

  • Rectangle Size: Represents the total number of collisions in that hour (larger rectangles = more collisions).
  • Color Intensity: Indicates the average delay duration (in minutes) for subway services. Darker colors = longer delays.

Key Observations:

  • Collision Frequency (Rectangle Size):
    • Hours like 15:00 (3 PM) and 14:00 (2 PM) are larger, reflecting higher collision counts.
    • These align with daytime rush or immediate post-rush periods.
  • Average Delay Duration (Color Intensity):
    • Darker rectangles (e.g., 5:00 AM and 7:00 AM) mean longer subway delays at relatively low collision hours.
    • Many high-collision hours (e.g., 15:00, 14:00) remain lighter, indicating shorter delays.
  • Overlap of High Collisions & Long Delays:
    • Few hours show both large size (high collisions) and dark color (long delays), implying weak correlation.
    • 5:00 AM has dark shading (long delays) but small size (low collisions), pointing to operational issues over external factors.
  • Off-Peak Hours:
    • Midnight, 3:00 AM, and 4:00 AM are small and light, reflecting minimal traffic activity and shorter delays.

Insights & Conclusions:

  • Weak Correlation: Large rectangles (high collisions) rarely overlap with dark colors (long delays), suggesting collisions do not majorly drive subway disruptions.
  • Operational Factors: Maintenance or equipment checks at early mornings or late evenings appear to be key delay drivers.
  • Focus on Off-Peak Interventions: The TTC could reduce delays by improving operational efficiency during hours with lower collision rates but higher delay durations.

Tools Used

  • SQL: Data extraction, cleaning, and transformation.
  • Tableau: Data visualization and interactive dashboard creation.

Conclusion: While traffic collisions are a notable urban concern, this analysis shows they have minimal direct impact on subway delays. Instead, addressing internal operational factors—such as maintenance schedules and equipment reliability—can significantly enhance TTC’s service efficiency.

Google Capstone Project: Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

As part of the Google Data Analytics Capstone, I focused on Cyclistic, a bike-share service in Chicago seeking to understand the differences in riding behavior between casual users and annual members. My goal was to identify strategies that could turn occasional riders into loyal subscribers, thereby boosting long-term revenue and user engagement.

Approach

  • Data Cleaning: Consolidated multiple CSV files in Excel, removed duplicates, and standardized columns (e.g., ride IDs, timestamps) for consistency.
  • Exploratory Data Analysis: Calculated average ride lengths, weekday/weekend usage frequencies, and monthly trends. Identified how seasonal factors influence ride volumes.
  • Visualization & Reporting: Built Tableau dashboards to compare casual vs. member ride lengths, usage distribution by day of the week, and seasonal patterns.

Key Findings

  • Longer, Leisure-Oriented Rides for Casual Users: Average ride length is ~24.65 minutes, almost double that of members (~12.67 minutes).
  • Weekend & Seasonal Surges: Casual riders show higher activity on weekends, especially during warmer months, while members maintain consistent weekday usage.

Purpose: Visualize the discrepancy in ride duration between casual riders and annual members.

Observation:

  • Casual riders average about 24.65 minutes per ride, indicating a more leisurely pace.
  • Members average around 12.67 minutes per ride, suggesting quick, utilitarian trips (e.g., commuting).

Insight: Casual users tend to treat the service as a recreational activity, whereas members integrate it into daily routines.

Additional Detail: This bar chart aggregates all recorded rides over the analysis period, then calculates the mean ride length by user type, highlighting a significant behavioral difference.

Purpose: Show how ridership fluctuates month by month for both user groups.

Observation:

  • Higher ride volumes occur in warmer months (May-August), especially among casual riders.
  • Members maintain steadier usage, although they also show an uptick during summer.

Insight: Seasonal promotions or incentives could capitalize on summertime interest to convert casual riders to membership plans.

Additional Detail: This chart enables a quick comparison of how weather and seasonal events affect both groups differently. Marketers can design campaigns targeting peak casual ridership months for maximum impact.

Purpose: Contrast how each user type spreads their ridership across days of the week.

Observation:

  • Casual riders’ usage spikes on weekends, implying leisure and tourism-driven behavior.
  • Members show consistent weekday patterns, aligning with routine commutes or daily errands.

Insight: Weekend-oriented promotions might appeal to casual riders, while commuter benefits and convenience features resonate with members.

Additional Detail: This visualization sums ride counts by weekday for each user group, exposing clear usage peaks on Saturdays and Sundays for casual users.

Tools Used

  • Excel: Data cleaning, preliminary exploration, and pivot tables.
  • Tableau: Dashboard creation for ride length comparison, seasonality trends, and usage patterns by day of week.

Conclusion: Targeted marketing campaigns during peak leisure periods (weekends and summer) could encourage casual riders to become annual members. Emphasizing membership perks—like unlimited rides, discounted rates, and priority service—would address the distinct needs of this recreational user base and potentially boost long-term retention.
