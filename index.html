Invocursor

A production SaaS platform democratizing enterprise-grade user onboarding. Built to provide an affordable alternative to tools like WalkMe ($78k/yr) for startups ($100/mo).

The Innovation: Integrated OpenAI GPT-4o with a custom lightweight JS widget to create a "Service + Software" model.

OpenAI API Node.js / Express Vanilla JS Widget Railway Deploy SaaS Business Model
View Live Demo

Executive Summary

Invocursor is a production SaaS platform that democratizes AI-powered user onboarding. It delivers enterprise-grade features at a fraction of the cost, using a "white-glove" AI configuration service to ensure reliability.

Technical Architecture

  • Backend: Node.js & Express REST API handling auth and rate limiting.
  • AI Engine: OpenAI GPT-4o-mini with mode-aware system prompts (Fast vs. Guided).
  • Frontend Widget: Zero-dependency Vanilla JS injected via script tag, ensuring isolation from client CSS/JS.
  • DevOps: Railway.app with GitHub CI/CD.

Key Challenges Solved

1. Cross-Domain Widget Injection

Problem: The widget must run on any client website without conflicting with their existing React/Vue/Angular frameworks.

Solution: Wrapped the widget in an IIFE (Immediately Invoked Function Expression) to prevent namespace pollution. Used Shadow DOM principles (simulated via strict CSS scoping) and high z-index management.

2. AI Cost Optimization

Problem: GPT-4 costs could erode margins.

Solution: Selected GPT-4o-mini ($0.0005/req). Implemented a weekly rate-limiting system that resets on Mondays. At 1500 requests/week, the cost is ~$3/mo per customer against a $100/mo subscription fee, yielding 97% gross margins.

Business Strategy: Instead of promising "fully automated" setup (which often fails on complex DOMs), I pivoted to a "Service + Software" model. The $199 setup fee covers the 1 hour of manual config I perform, creating a defensible moat and ensuring customer success.

Project Overview

BrainBites creates a virtuous cycle: reducing screen time earns quiz opportunities, and learning earns time back. It is currently live-ready (APK/AAB) with a full monetization strategy.

The AI Advantage

I utilized Claude Code to compress a typical 6-12 month learning curve into 3 months. The AI acted as a pair programmer, helping architect the bridge between React Native JavaScript and Native Android Kotlin code.

Technical Complexity

Native Android Services

React Native alone cannot track device-wide usage. I wrote custom Kotlin modules to access the Android UsageStatsManager API. This required implementing a persistent Foreground Service to prevent the OS from killing the timer.

State Synchronization

Synchronizing the "Real-time" Native timer with the "Reactive" JS UI was the hardest challenge. I implemented an event-emitter bridge that pushes updates from Kotlin to the Zustand store in React Native, ensuring the UI always reflects the true system state.

Operational Analytics

Urban Transportation: TTC Collision & Delay Correlation

Abstract: Analyzing 250,000+ collision records and 75,000 subway delays to test the hypothesis that surface traffic impacts underground reliability. Used SQL for geospatial joins and Tableau for multi-dimensional temporal analysis.
Advanced SQL Tableau Geospatial Analysis Statistical Regression

Executive Summary

The Toronto Transit Commission (TTC) serves 1.8M daily riders. This study investigated whether surface-level traffic collisions correlate with underground subway delays. The business implication: should investment focus on traffic management or internal equipment maintenance?

Data Architecture

  • Sources: City of Toronto Open Data (Collision DB) & TTC Operational DB.
  • Preparation: SQL spatial joins (PostGIS) linking collision coordinates to subway station proximity zones (500m radius).
  • Cleaning: Handled 12k+ records with missing timestamps using systematic imputation.

Key Findings

1. Statistical Independence

Contrary to intuitive assumptions, surface collisions and subway delays are statistically independent. Pearson correlation analysis yielded ρ = 0.12 (p=0.58), indicating no significant relationship.

2. Temporal Divergence

  • Collisions: Peak at 8 AM and 5 PM (Rush Hour).
  • Subway Delays: Peak at 6 AM (Equipment cold-starts) and 10 PM (Operational fatigue).

This "Rush Hour Paradox" reveals that despite high traffic chaos at 5 PM, subway delays are below average, likely due to full staffing and resource deployment.

Recommendation: Redirect analytical focus away from traffic patterns. The 6 AM delay peak (Equipment Issues) suggests the need for predictive maintenance on aging infrastructure, not traffic management.

Marketing Analytics

Cyclistic Bike-Share: User Segmentation Strategy

Abstract: Analysis of 5.7 million ride records to drive subscription conversion. Identified distinct behavioral clusters between "Casual" riders and "Annual Members" to optimize marketing spend.
R / Python Clustering Analysis Tableau Behavioral Modeling

Business Context

Cyclistic wants to convert casual riders (high churn) into annual members (high LTV). The goal was to find behavioral triggers that indicate a casual rider is ready to convert.

Methodology

  • Data Processing: Aggregated 12 months of ride data (Jan-Dec 2023).
  • Feature Engineering: Created variables for ride_length, commute_hours (7-9am, 5-7pm), and weekend_ratio.
  • Segmentation: K-means clustering to identify sub-groups within the "Casual" dataset.

Key Insights

1. The "Flexible Commuter" Segment: 22% of casual riders exhibit "Member-like" behavior (short, weekday, point-to-point rides) but pay per ride. These are the highest probability conversion targets.

2. The Weekend Gap: Casual riders ride 2x longer (24 min avg) than members (12 min avg), primarily on weekends. This indicates casual usage is experiential/leisure, while member usage is utilitarian/commute.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Target the "Flexible Commuter": Send push notifications to casual users who ride >3 times M-F during rush hour. Messaging: "You're already commuting like a member. Save $40/mo by switching."
  • Summer Breakeven Campaign: Identify casual riders whose monthly spend exceeds the membership fee ($16). Alert them: "You've crossed the breakeven point. Next rides could be free."
Financial Risk Analysis

Meme Stocks vs. Safe Havens: A Volatility Study

Abstract: A 5-year comparative study (2020-2025) of speculative assets (GME, AMC) vs. stable assets (Gold, Walmart). Demonstrates how visualization methodology impacts investment decision-making.
Python (Pandas) Time-Series Analysis Risk Modeling

Research Question

How do the risk-return profiles of "Meme Stocks" compare to traditional defensive assets during market regime shifts (COVID-19, Inflation)?

Analytical Framework

I utilized normalized returns (Growth of $1) rather than absolute price to enable fair comparison. Metrics calculated included Sharpe Ratio, Sortino Ratio, and Maximum Drawdown.

Key Findings

  • Volatility Clustering: GME and AMC exhibit extreme volatility clustering (Autocorrelation ≈ 0.62). 5 years of gains were generated in ~15 trading days.
  • Risk-Adjusted Performance: Walmart (WMT) delivered a Sharpe Ratio of 1.12 vs. GME's -0.15. Despite the hype, the "boring" asset generated superior wealth preservation.
  • Correlation Analysis: Meme stocks showed near-zero correlation with Gold (ρ = 0.05), confirming they are driven by idiosyncratic social sentiment, not macro-economic hedging.

Conclusion

The study proves that while meme stocks offer lottery-like payoffs, they destroy portfolio efficiency. A portfolio rebalanced monthly with 40% WMT / 40% Gold / 20% Speculative would have reduced drawdown by 60% compared to a pure speculative portfolio.
